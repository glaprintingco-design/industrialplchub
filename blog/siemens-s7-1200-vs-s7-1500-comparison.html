Siemens S7-1200 vs S7-1500: Which TIA Portal PLC Fits Your Application?

Overview

When considering Siemens PLC options, the S7-1200 and S7-1500 series are frequently compared by plant managers and controls engineers. Each series offers distinct advantages in terms of performance, integration, and cost. Understanding these differences is crucial for selecting the right PLC for your application, especially when using TIA Portal, Siemens' integrated automation software suite.

S7-1200 Specifications

The S7-1200 series is designed for smaller automation tasks but is powerful enough to handle complex processes. It's best suited for applications requiring compact size and scalability.
Specification Details
CPU Memory 50 KB Program, 2 MB Work
Scan Time 60 µs per 1k instructions
I/O Capacity Up to 8 modules
Communication Ports Ethernet, PROFINET
Voltage 24V DC
Current Draw 160 mA
Engineering Tip:

The S7-1200 is ideal for distributed control systems where space is at a premium. Consider the Siemens 6ES7 215-1AG40-0XB0 Simatic S7-1200 Central Processor Module for enhanced network capabilities.

S7-1500 Specifications

The S7-1500 series is tailored for more demanding applications, offering higher performance and advanced features like integrated motion control.
Specification Details
CPU Memory 512 KB Program, 5 MB Work
Scan Time 10 µs per 1k instructions
I/O Capacity Up to 32 modules
Communication Ports Ethernet, PROFINET, PROFIBUS DP
Voltage 120/230V AC
Current Draw 350 mA
Warning:

When transitioning from an S7-1200 to an S7-1500, ensure compatibility with existing peripherals and network configurations to avoid unexpected downtime.

Key Differences Between S7-1200 and S7-1500

The primary differences between the S7-1200 and S7-1500 lie in their processing power, I/O capacity, and communication options. The S7-1500's advanced capabilities make it suitable for complex, high-speed applications, whereas the S7-1200 is more cost-effective for simpler tasks.

Integration with TIA Portal

Both PLC series integrate seamlessly with TIA Portal, allowing for efficient project management, from hardware configuration to programming and diagnostics. TIA Portal supports multiple programming standards such as ladder logic and structured text per IEC 61131-3.

Real-World Considerations

Selecting between the S7-1200 and S7-1500 should factor in future scalability, budget constraints, and existing infrastructure. Downtime risk and the availability of spare parts should guide the decision, especially in industries like automotive or food & beverage where the cost of downtime can be significant.
Engineering Tip:

For systems where end-of-life parts are a concern, consider the migration path and spare parts strategy. While the Siemens SIMATIC S7-1200 6ES7214-1AG40-0XB0 offers a cost-effective solution, the S7-1500 may provide longer-term value.
